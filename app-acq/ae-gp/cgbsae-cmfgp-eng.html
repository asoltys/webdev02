Commodity Management Framework and Green Procurement

The Commodity Management Framework has been established to review, plan, acquire and control the total life cycle activities of commonly procured goods and services:

  • Meeting operational requirements of government departments;
  • Providing the optimal cost of ownership and disposal; and
  • Achieving the best value for Canada

To achieve the above referenced objectives, Pre-Competed Procurement Instruments are awarded upon completion of commodity processes, the outcome of which is documented in a Commodity Plan. The development of a Green Procurement Plan that specifically outlines how environmental considerations will be addressed in current and future procurement instruments is a mandatory part of the Commodity Plan. A Commodity Management Quality Assurance process is currently in place to ensure that procurement instruments awarded through Commodity Management processes include environmental specifications to the greatest extent possible. Acquisitions Branch's Green Procurement Team, the Office of Greening Government Operations (OGGO), Environment Canada and Natural Resources Canada all contribute environmental and sustainability expertise to this process.

There is a tracking system in place for Green Procurement Plans and it is continually maintained by Acquisitions Branch's Green Procurement Team in order to provide ongoing, up-to-date information on the progress of implementing the Policy on Green Procurement for a given commodity.
